Open Source vs Free Software

A fundamental difference

Free Software

A matter of liberty, not price.

Free as in free speech, not as in free beer.

Four essential freedoms:

  • Freedom to run the program as you wish, for any purpose
  • Freedom to study how the program works, and change it so it does your computing as you wish
  • Freedom to redistribute copies so you can help your neighbor
  • Freedom to distribute copies of your modified version to others

The Free Software Foundation

Aims to promote the Free Software movement.

Non-profit organization founded in 1985 by Richard Stallman (GCC, GDB, Emacs, ...)

Open Source

Focuses on how the software is developed and distributed, shadows the philosophical and political meaning.

More politically correct for companies.

The Open Source Initiative

Organization devoted to promote Open Source Software (OSS).

Funded in 1998 from a schism in the Free Software community.

Inspire to drive a policy deemed more adequate to technic and economic realities.

The License!

A very important, but vehemently discussed and confusing part of open sourcing products.

Why does it matters so much?

  • You protect yourelf
  • You define the level of re-use
  • You give a cear message who can contribute

Some examples:

  • GNU GPLv3: licensed works and modifications must be under the same license
  • MIT License: derivated works must retain the original copyright and license notices, but may be licensed under different terms
  • The Unlicense: full dedication to public domain
